Computer Integrated Manufacturing

What is Computer Integrated Manufacturing?

Manufactured items are part of everyday life, yet most students have not been introduced to the high-tech, innovative nature of modern manufacturing. This course illuminates the opportunities related to understanding manufacturing. At the same time, it teaches students about manufacturing processes, product design, robotics, and automation. Students learn to use flowchart programming and G&M procedures, which are industry standards for this field. Additionaly students design a processes prior to testing to maximize budget efficiency.

Instructors Mr. Konecny

What will I learn after I take Computer Integrated Manufacturing?

  • After taking the class students will have/be able to:


  • - Learn VEX

  • - Control mechanics and robotics

  • - Mill a block of wood using G&M code

  • - Apply manufacturing standards to their own designs

  • - Maintain detailed records of budget and design constraints
