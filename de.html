Digital Electronics

What is Digital Electronics?

Digital Electronics is extremely unique when compared to the rest of the engineering courses at the Science Academy. Instead of receiving an introduction to the course, students immediately dive into several key components such as "Multisim" and "Breadboarding". Multisim is a circuit design software used to test a circuit before actually making one, also known as breadboarding. Over the course of time, the students acquire experiences and proceed to learn Boolean Algebra and Logic Design (the process of having the circuit take decisions), with the objective of simplifying and enhancing their circuits.

Instructors Mrs. Houston, Mr. Rodriguez

What will I learn after I take Digital Electronics

  • After taking the class students will have the ability to:


  • - Understand the basics of the Boolean algebra

  • - Recognize how to solve problems with logic design

  • - Solder resistors, and diodes and other circuit components in a neat fashion

  • - Efficiently use Multisim to simulate designed circuits

  • - Design and Breadboard Circuits
